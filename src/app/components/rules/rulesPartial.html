Official Rules of NWA Fantasy - EURO 2016

About

This fantasy game application is for managing predictions and subsequent points for UEFA Euro Cup 2016.

Registration

Registration deadline for creating a player account is July 10th, 2016.

Prediction rules

  • Please make sure that you refresh the browser and can see your submission in the 'Prediction List' after predicting.
  • Match predictions will be available no later than 12 hours before the next match (or after completion of last match of the previous day, whichever is later).
  • Match predictions will be scored in the following way on completion of the match:
    • Regular Matches (06/10/2016 - 06/22/2016)
      • Win - 3 points
      • Loss - 0 points
    • Round of 16 (06/25/2016 - 06/27/2016)
      • Win - 6 points
      • Loss - 0 points
    • Quarter finals (06/30/2016 - 07/03/2016)
      • Win - 9 points
      • Loss - 0 points
    • Semi finals (07/06/2016 - 07/07/2016)
      • Win - 12 points
      • Loss - 0 points
    • Final (07/10/2016)
      • Win - 15 points
      • Loss - 0 points
  • Prediction from other players will be hidden starting with the Quarterfinal phase. Other player's predictions will be visible once prediction has been locked 15 minutes prior to the game.
  • A draw match is considered a loss for a player unless said player has predicted a draw.
  • A player will be awarded win points if player predicts a draw and match ends in a draw.
  • There will be no DRAW option after the Round of 16 matches, starting on Jun 25th, 2016.
  • Total points for the day will be awarded after the completion of the last match of the day.

Prediction Lockdown

  • Predictions for each match will be locked 15 minutes prior to the match.
  • If a player misses a prediction, he/she will forfeit the points for that match.

  • If multiple players are contending for any of the above places, the following rules will apply:
    • The players tied in a particular position will share that spot. 
    • The position of the player with the next highest point will be pushed down by the number of players sharing that spot.
    • Let's consider two examples with A, B, C, D and E playing.

Example 1

A - 100
B - 100
C - 80
D - 80
E - 60

A and B are joint firsts. There will be no second place. C and D are joint thirds.
A and B will be entitled to the sum of first and second place prize money.
C and D will be entitled to the third place prize money.

Example 2

A - 100
B - 100
C - 100
D - 80
E - 60

A, B and C are joint firsts. There will be no second or third places.
A, B and C will be entitled to the sum of first, second and third place prize money.

  • In both the examples, how the winners decide to share the loot, is up to them. They can divide them equally or use any other method to solve this problem.
